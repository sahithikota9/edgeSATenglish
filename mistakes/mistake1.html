Mistake 1 – Common SAT English Errors

← Back to Home

1. Subject-Verb Agreement

Example: The list of items are on the desk.
Correction: The list of items is on the desk.

Tip: Identify the subject of the sentence, not a phrase that comes between the subject and verb. Singular subjects need singular verbs; plural subjects need plural verbs.

2. Misplaced Modifier

Example: She almost drove her kids to school every day.
Correction: She drove her kids to school almost every day.

Tip: Place descriptive words/phrases close to the word they modify. Misplaced modifiers can make sentences confusing or change the meaning.

3. Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement

Example: Every student must submit their homework.
Correction: Every student must submit his or her homework.

Tip: Pronouns must agree in number with the nouns they replace. “Everyone,” “each,” “every student” are singular, so use “his or her” instead of “their.”

4. Wrong Verb Tense

Example: He will went to the store yesterday.
Correction: He went to the store yesterday.

Tip: Make sure past, present, and future tenses are used consistently. “Yesterday” signals past tense, so “went” is correct, not “will go.”

5. Dangling Modifier

Example: Walking down the street, the trees were beautiful.
Correction: Walking down the street, I saw the beautiful trees.

Tip: Ensure the subject performing the action is clear. Dangling modifiers occur when the introductory phrase does not clearly refer to the subject of the main clause.
