Mistake 3 – Common SAT English Errors

← Back to Home

1. Incorrect Comparisons

Incorrect: She is more smarter than me.
Correct: She is smarter than I am.
Tip: Avoid double comparatives (“more smarter” → “smarter”).

2. Double Negatives

Incorrect: I don’t need no help.
Correct: I don’t need any help.
Tip: Two negatives make a positive; use only one.

3. Wrong Preposition

Incorrect: She is good in math.
Correct: She is good at math.
Tip: Common SAT prepositions: good at, interested in, different from.

4. Redundancy

Incorrect: He returned back home.
Correct: He returned home.
Tip: Avoid using unnecessary words that repeat the meaning.

5. Wrong Word Form

Incorrect: She did good on the test.
Correct: She did well on the test.
Tip: "Good" is an adjective; "well" is an adverb describing actions.
