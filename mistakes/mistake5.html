Mistake 5 – Common SAT English Errors

← Back to Home

1. Inconsistent Verb Tense

Incorrect: Yesterday I go to the store.
Correct: Yesterday I went to the store.
Tip: Match verb tense to time markers (yesterday → past).

2. Wrong Preposition

Incorrect: He is good on math.
Correct: He is good at math.
Tip: Certain adjectives are always paired with specific prepositions.

3. Subject Pronoun vs Object Pronoun

Incorrect: Him and I went to the store.
Correct: He and I went to the store.
Tip: Use subject pronouns for subjects, object pronouns for objects.

4. Improper Parallelism

Incorrect: She likes reading, to swim, and jogging.
Correct: She likes reading, swimming, and jogging.
Tip: Make sure elements in a series match in grammatical form.

5. Incorrect Idiomatic Expression

Incorrect: He is married with a doctor.
Correct: He is married to a doctor.
Tip: Learn common SAT idioms (married to, differ from, interested in).
