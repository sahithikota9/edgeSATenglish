Passage

In the early 20th century, the city of Detroit experienced rapid growth due to the booming automobile industry. Many immigrants came seeking employment, contributing to a vibrant but crowded urban environment. Amidst this expansion, challenges such as inadequate housing and limited public services arose.

Words in Context Tips

  • Boiling → fast growth, "booming industry" means rapidly expanding.
  • Amidst → surrounded by; used to indicate circumstances.
  • Inadequate → insufficient, not enough.
  • Vibrant → lively, full of energy.
  • Urban → related to cities.

Tip: Read sentences around the word carefully to infer meaning; eliminate extreme options first.

Common Errors

1. Misreading Context

Always read the sentence before and after the target word to infer meaning.

2. Ignoring Transition Words

Words like "however," "amidst," "thus" change the meaning; do not skip them.

3. Confusing Similar Words

Ensure you know subtle differences: urban vs. suburban, vibrancy vs. vitality.

4. Overlooking Sentence Structure

Complex sentences can mislead; parse subject, verb, and modifiers carefully.

5. Ignoring Tone

Words in context may carry positive, negative, or neutral tone; match answer accordingly.
