Mistake 8 – Common SAT English Errors

← Back to Home

1. Faulty Comparison

Incorrect: She is smarter than anyone in the class.
Correct: She is smarter than anyone else in the class.
Tip: Comparisons need a clear standard of comparison (anyone else, not just anyone).

2. Redundant Expression

Incorrect: Repeat again the instructions.
Correct: Repeat the instructions.
Tip: Avoid unnecessary words that repeat meaning.

3. Wrong Pronoun Reference

Incorrect: When John spoke to Mike, he was angry.
Correct: When John spoke to Mike, John was angry.
Tip: Ensure pronouns clearly refer to the intended subject.

4. Improper Use of Colons

Incorrect: I bought: apples, bananas, and oranges.
Correct: I bought apples, bananas, and oranges.
Tip: Colons introduce a list or explanation; don’t place one immediately after a verb.

5. Incorrect Idiom Usage

Incorrect: He is good in tennis.
Correct: He is good at tennis.
Tip: Idioms are fixed expressions; learn the correct prepositions.
