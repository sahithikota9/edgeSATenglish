Mistake 6 – Common SAT English Errors

← Back to Home

1. Improper Adjective Order

Incorrect: She bought a red beautiful dress.
Correct: She bought a beautiful red dress.
Tip: Use correct adjective order: opinion → size → age → color → origin → material.

2. Double Comparatives

Incorrect: This problem is more easier than the last.
Correct: This problem is easier than the last.
Tip: Avoid using "more" or "most" with adjectives ending in -er/-est.

3. Confusing Homophones

Incorrect: I except your apology.
Correct: I accept your apology.
Tip: Learn commonly tested homophones (accept/except, affect/effect).

4. Incorrect Subjunctive

Incorrect: I wish I was taller.
Correct: I wish I were taller.
Tip: Use subjunctive "were" for hypothetical or contrary-to-fact statements.

5. Misplaced Modifiers

Incorrect: He almost drove his kids to school every day.
Correct: He drove his kids to school almost every day.
Tip: Place modifiers next to the word they describe to avoid confusion.
